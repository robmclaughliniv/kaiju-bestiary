# Guild Classification System

**Canon status:** Active standard, revised AE 2287

The Natural Phenomena Preservation Society classifies kaiju by origin, disposition, and threat index. These fields describe what the Guild believes, not necessarily what is objectively true.

## Origin classes

- **Primordial:** Ancient macro-organisms associated with planetary processes.
- **Botanical:** Plantlike, fungal, or photosynthetic organisms and ecologies.
- **Mineral:** Organisms whose bodies are substantially lithic, crystalline, or metallic.
- **Echo:** Entities whose primary effects involve resonance, memory, cognition, or information.
- **Astral:** Organisms associated with the upper atmosphere, orbital phenomena, or nonterrestrial cycles.
- **Colonial:** A superorganism composed of many interdependent organisms.
- **Abyssal:** Deep-ocean organisms adapted to extreme pressure and darkness.
- **Artificial:** Constructed, altered, cultivated, or manufactured entities.
- **Symbiotic:** Entities inseparable from a host species, culture, structure, or ecosystem.
- **Unknown:** Evidence is insufficient or mutually contradictory.

Multiple origins may be assigned when no single category is adequate.

## Disposition

- **Passive:** Does not deliberately pursue or displace observers under normal conditions.
- **Migratory:** Movement follows a route, cycle, resource, signal, or unknown call.
- **Territorial:** Responds strongly to intrusion within a stable range.
- **Catastrophic:** Its ordinary biological activity predictably destroys existing ecosystems or settlements.
- **Undetermined:** Available observations do not support a reliable behavioral model.

Disposition is not morality. A passive kaiju may still kill millions by existing.

## Threat index

Threat measures probable long-term ecological and civilizational impact.

| Level | Meaning |
|---|---|
| None | No demonstrated macro-scale harm |
| Low | Localized and recoverable disruption |
| Moderate | Regional danger requiring organized response |
| High | Severe regional losses or persistent ecological change |
| Extreme | Multi-region collapse likely without intervention |
| Existential | Threatens civilization, biosphere stability, or planetary continuity |

Legacy dossiers may use letter grades. Approximate translation: C = Moderate, B = High, A = Extreme, S = Catastrophic or Existential depending on context.

## Record confidence

Each factual claim should eventually carry one of four confidence states:

- **Verified:** Confirmed through repeatable observation or physical evidence.
- **Probable:** Strongly supported but incompletely tested.
- **Disputed:** Credible Guild authorities disagree.
- **Apocryphal:** Preserved for cultural or investigative value without reliable confirmation.
