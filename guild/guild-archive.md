# The Guild Archive

**Formal name:** Natural Phenomena Preservation Society  
**Common name:** The Guild  
**Archive mark:** NPPS  
**Founding charter:** AE 217

## Mandate

The Guild exists to observe, record, preserve, and interpret phenomena too large, ancient, dangerous, or culturally consequential for ordinary institutions. Its founding creed is commonly rendered as:

> To observe. To record. To understand. Not to conquer, nor to profit, but to preserve the knowledge of that which walks beyond the scale of nations.

The Guild does not officially classify kaiju as enemies, gods, or property. Individual branches and researchers often fail to live up to that principle.

## Five principles

1. Knowledge before force.
2. Observation before judgment.
3. Record without distortion.
4. Protect life when possible.
5. Leave no trace but footprints.

## Major branches

- **Aurelian Headquarters:** Central archive, taxonomy council, diplomatic authority.
- **Xenor Outpost:** Western field operations and continental interiors.
- **Stratosphere Station:** Upper-atmosphere observation and Echo research.
- **Abyssal Observatory:** Deep-ocean expeditions and pressure-zone biology.
- **Polar Watch:** Northern and southern migration monitoring.

## Field records

A standard dossier contains classification, habitat, scale estimate, known behavior, ecological effects, abilities, cultural records, observation logs, and researcher commentary. Marginalia is preserved when authorship can be authenticated.

The archive is living, not infallible. Later observations may contradict earlier records. Contradictions remain visible so future researchers can understand how knowledge changed.

## Archive tone

Guild writing is precise but never fully detached. Researchers have watched cities vanish, heard impossible songs, and built careers around creatures they may only glimpse once. The archive should feel like natural history written under conditions of awe, grief, obsession, and institutional pressure.

## High Archivist's warning

> These pages are not a complete truth. They are a collecting of moments, fleeting, fractured, and fragile, offered by those who dared to stand before the impossible and return.
>
> We do not seek to tame them. We seek to remember them.
