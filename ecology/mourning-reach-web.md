# Mourning Reach Ecological Web

## Current model

The Mourning Reach is treated as a network of interacting colossal organisms rather than a collection of independent threats.

### Bloomwraith
- consumes carrion and contaminated remains
- concentrates Venomvine toxins in root-nodules
- reduces disease after mass casualty events
- may erase evidence needed to understand die-offs

### Venomvine
- controls routes through Widowfen
- kills or redirects large herbivores
- creates protected pools for resistant species
- spreads through water and fragmentation

### Orchidia
- appears before regional ecological shifts
- may alter Venomvine toxin production
- moves through Bloomwraith colonies without resistance
- may coordinate, negotiate with, or exploit the wider ecosystem

## Known interactions

| Source | Target | Relationship | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Venomvine | Large wetland fauna | Predation and movement control | High |
| Bloomwraith | Venomvine-killed fauna | Carrion processing | High |
| Orchidia | Venomvine | Chemical or behavioral signaling | Medium |
| Orchidia | Bloomwraith | Non-aggression or recognition | Medium |
| Human settlements | Venomvine | Harvest, cultivation, accidental spread | High |
| Guild intervention | Entire Reach | Frequent cascade risk | High |

## Missing organisms

Phase One requires additional entries to make the region feel complete:

1. a large herbivore whose migration is shaped by Venomvine
2. a small commensal species that lives inside Bloomwraith colonies
3. an aerial pollinator capable of crossing the Quiet Interior
4. an apex predator not based on plant anatomy
5. a human-domesticated organism adapted to pollen storms

## Design rule

Every new entry added to the Mourning Reach must modify at least two existing relationships in this file.
