# Canon Rules

## Canon levels

### Locked canon
Facts that define the identity of the setting. Changing them requires an explicit retcon entry.

### Working canon
Ideas currently treated as true but still open to refinement.

### Field claim
An in-world observation, rumor, theory, or testimony that may be incomplete or false.

### Non-canon
Discarded explorations retained only for production history.

## Rules of the world

1. Kaiju are organisms, forces, colonies, or living systems—not merely oversized animals.
2. Every major creature must have an ecological role, geographic range, and relationship to human life.
3. No power exists solely because it looks impressive. Abilities require anatomy, behavior, symbiosis, environmental interaction, or a deliberately preserved mystery.
4. The Guild is fallible. Its records may conflict, contain propaganda, or reflect obsolete science.
5. Civilizations adapt to kaiju through architecture, ritual, trade, migration, technology, and taboo.
6. Defeating a kaiju is rarely equivalent to solving the problem it represents.
7. Scale must remain legible. Entries should use comparisons, aftermath, tracks, displaced landscapes, and human infrastructure to communicate size.
8. New canon should create connections to existing canon wherever possible.

## Retcon protocol

A retcon must record:

- the original claim
- the replacement claim
- the reason for the change
- affected files
- whether the old claim remains as an in-world misconception

## Naming protocol

Each creature may have:

- a Guild catalog number
- a standardized Guild name
- one or more regional names
- a scientific or taxonomic placeholder
- titles used by cults, soldiers, traders, or survivors

Names should reveal who is speaking.
