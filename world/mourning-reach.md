# The Mourning Reach

**Canon status:** Working canon  
**Region type:** Poisoned highland basin, drowned forest, and orchid-choked escarpment  
**Primary Guild designation:** Survey Region MR-7

## Overview

The Mourning Reach is a vast inland depression where blackwater wetlands climb into broken limestone terraces and cloud forests. Seasonal fog carries pollen, spores, and chemical signals between organisms on a scale the Guild does not fully understand. Travelers often describe the region as a single enormous garden. Veteran naturalists reject that metaphor: gardens have gardeners, borders, and an intended design.

The Reach appears to have none of those things.

## Why it matters

The region is the first major proof that kaiju are not isolated anomalies. Bloom cycles, migrations, predation, soil chemistry, and settlement history all interlock. Killing one organism can trigger consequences hundreds of kilometers away.

## Major subregions

### The Violet Escarpment
A stepped wall of mineral-rich limestone where enormous root systems split cliffs into hanging islands. Orchidia sightings cluster here during the wet season.

### Widowfen
A drowned forest threaded with mats of venomous vine tissue. Entire villages are built on mobile platforms to avoid root contact.

### The Pale Orchard
A field of white, tree-like growths believed to be shed structures from Bloomwraith colonies. No living canopy remains, yet the orchard flowers after distant thunder.

### Saint Orra's Road
A raised stone causeway connecting the Reach's surviving trade settlements. Its oldest sections predate all known regional states and are too broad for human traffic alone.

### The Quiet Interior
A cartographic blank where compasses drift, birds refuse to cross, and Guild expeditions lose chronological agreement in their journals.

## Human presence

Settlements in the Reach rely on elevated construction, sealed cisterns, pollen bells, burn corridors, and seasonal evacuation rites. Local knowledge frequently outperforms Guild doctrine, though the Guild has historically dismissed it as superstition.

## Core mysteries

- Why do unrelated organisms synchronize their bloom cycles?
- What produced the road beneath Saint Orra's Road?
- Why do historical maps show the Quiet Interior in different locations?
- Is the Reach an ecosystem, a superorganism, or a battlefield whose combatants move too slowly for humans to recognize?
