# Bestiary No.001: Gravorax

**Guild epithet:** The Earth That Walks  
**Japanese display name:** グラヴォラックス  
**Field designation:** 001-A  
**Canon status:** Established

## Classification

- **Origin:** Primordial / Mineral
- **Guild descriptor:** Terrastomp Colossus
- **Disposition:** Migratory
- **Threat:** Extreme
- **First verified record:** AE 2147
- **Primary habitat:** Mountain ranges, fault zones, uplift belts

## Scale

- **Estimated length:** 138 m
- **Estimated mass:** 112,000 t
- **Locomotion:** Quadrupedal, compression-driven

## Morphology

Gravorax resembles a mountain predator assembled from interlocking plates of metamorphic stone. Its dorsal ridge contains blue crystalline pressure organs that brighten before major movement or shockwave discharge. These structures are not decorative mineral growths. They appear to store mechanical stress gathered through the creature's limbs and ventral plates.

Its head is low, armored, and wedge-shaped. The forelimbs are disproportionately massive and function as both locomotor structures and seismic hammers. The body sheds stone continuously, making tissue boundaries difficult to distinguish from accumulated debris.

## Ecology and behavior

Gravorax follows active geological systems rather than prey. It detects distant seismic vibrations and alters course toward regions of accumulating crustal stress. Its passage destroys roads, settlements, and mountain ecosystems, but may also release pressure that would otherwise produce larger earthquakes.

This has created the central dispute around No.001: whether Gravorax causes disasters or performs a planetary regulatory function whose local cost is mistaken for aggression.

It does not appear to feed conventionally. Probable energy sources include piezoelectric conversion, heat exchange, and mineral absorption.

## Recorded abilities

### Earthbreak Stomp
A forelimb impact that produces a radial ground wave capable of collapsing fortifications and exposing buried fault lines.

### Rock-Horn Charge
A direct, accelerating advance through obstacles. Gravorax does not maneuver well at speed.

### Compression Build
Stores mechanical pressure within dorsal crystal organs. Prolonged storage is visible as intensifying blue light and increasing local tremors.

### Tectonic Release
A massive shockwave generated when accumulated pressure is discharged through the body. The exact upper limit is unknown.

## Guild advisory

Do not approach from valleys, passes, tunnels, bridges, or engineered slopes. Evacuation routes must run laterally from the projected migration line, never ahead of it.

Seismic silence is not evidence that Gravorax has departed. The organism has remained motionless beneath rockfall for as long as eleven days.

## Cultural record

Highland clans describe Gravorax as the anger of the land awakening. Several traditions forbid rebuilding directly in its path, not from fear alone, but from the belief that repeated passage marks a place the earth itself intends to reclaim.

> When the mountains walk and the earth trembles, the anger of the land awakens. We must never cross its path.

## Researcher's comment

> Its movements are not acts of destruction, but part of the planet's rebalancing. Maintain distance and avoid its path at all costs.

## Open questions

- Are the dorsal crystals organs, symbionts, or cultivated mineral structures?
- Does Gravorax select stressed faults deliberately?
- Is No.001 unique, or merely the first member of a buried population?
- Why does it occasionally stop near ancient stone settlements with no measurable seismic activity?
