# Bestiary No.086: Thalassion

**Guild epithet:** The Cathedral Below  
**Japanese display name:** タラシオン  
**Field designation:** 086-A  
**Canon status:** Established

## Classification

- **Origin:** Abyssal / Colonial
- **Guild descriptor:** Abyssal Cathedral Colossus
- **Disposition:** Migratory
- **Threat:** Catastrophic
- **First verified record:** AE 1503
- **Primary habitat:** Deep ocean, continental shelf edges, fog-bound migration routes

## Scale

- **Estimated length:** At least 1,480 m
- **Estimated mass:** Indeterminable
- **Locomotion:** Deep-current propulsion and slow surface drift

## Morphology

Thalassion is an abyssal superorganism whose surfaced dorsal mass resembles a dark island crowned by a ruined cathedral-city. Towers, arches, buttresses, bridges, and windowlike cavities rise from armored tissue. Blue light glows through many openings beneath the waterline.

The structures are confirmed to be biological in at least some regions, but their architectural regularity remains unexplained. Samples recovered after shedding contain shell, nerve tissue, mineral lattice, and traces of smaller organisms living within vascular chambers.

A single immense eye has been observed below the apparent shoreline. Other eye reports remain disputed.

## Ecology and behavior

Thalassion spends months or years below recorded depth, then rises in heavy fog and remains nearly motionless for days or weeks. Sailors mistake its back for an island, harbor, abandoned city, or sanctuary.

The organism supports a dense colonial ecosystem. Some inhabitants appear symbiotic. Others may be parasites, scavengers, or independent species. Bells are frequently reported during surfacing events, although no mechanical bell has been recovered.

After prolonged stillness, Thalassion descends gradually. In rare cases it submerges without warning, producing violent currents and mass marine mortality.

## Recorded abilities

### False Shore
Creates the appearance of stable land by remaining motionless and altering local currents, fog, and wave behavior.

### Bells of the Deep
Produces low-frequency resonance perceived as distant bells. The sound interferes with navigation, sleep, and temporal judgment.

### Gradual Submergence
Descends with deceptive slowness, causing structures and landing parties to misjudge the remaining time.

### Cathedral Host
Supports numerous organisms within and upon its dorsal architecture. The total colony may be essential to Thalassion's survival.

### Returning Current
Generates powerful currents capable of pulling ships, debris, and marine life toward its migration path.

## Guild advisory

Landing on unidentified islands is prohibited until tidal movement has been observed for no fewer than three days. Fog-bound landforms must be surveyed by sonar before approach.

Do not follow bells through dense fog.

## Cultural record

Coastal peoples call Thalassion the Sea Cathedral. Songs describe a drowned congregation that still gathers beneath its towers. Some communities leave empty boats offshore as offerings. Others extinguish every bell during seasonal fog.

> From the ballad Song of the Tidal Bells: The shore rose from the mist, and by dawn the shore had breathed.

## Researcher's comment

> What we see is not architecture. It is a mind that wears stone. We thought we studied the ocean. In truth, it studies us when it washes.

## Open questions

- Are the cathedral structures evolved, cultivated, inherited, or inhabited?
- What produces the reported bells?
- Are people or intelligent nonhuman organisms living inside the dorsal city?
- Why do maps recovered from vanished expeditions show streets that match later surfacing events?
- Is Thalassion one organism, a colony, or a mobile ecosystem using a shared body?
