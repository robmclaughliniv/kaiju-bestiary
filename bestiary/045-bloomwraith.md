# Bestiary No.045 — Bloomwraith

**Canon status:** Working canon  
**Operational class:** Class III — Evacuate  
**Known range:** The Pale Orchard and western Mourning Reach  
**Primary ecology:** Mobile floral colony / carrion recycler

## Identification

Bloomwraiths resemble towering humanoid figures at a distance, but the silhouette is produced by hundreds of interlocked stalks surrounding a hollow central cavity. Their pale blossoms open only around recent death. At dusk, drifting petals create the illusion of robes moving against the wind.

## Biology

A Bloomwraith is not a single body. It is a coordinated colony that assembles around a mobile root-knot. The colony incorporates bone, shell, wood, and mineral fragments as temporary structural material. No brain has been identified; coordination may occur through chemical pulses and vibration.

## Behavior

Bloomwraiths follow battlefields, mass die-offs, and kaiju feeding grounds. They are usually indifferent to healthy animals but become violent when prevented from reaching carrion. Witnesses report that wounded individuals hear familiar voices from inside the colony. The Guild has not determined whether this is mimicry, hallucination, or folklore.

## Ecological role

They rapidly break down contaminated remains and concentrate toxins into pearl-like root nodules. This prevents some disease outbreaks but can also erase evidence of what killed an ecosystem.

## Field guidance

Do not shelter among pale blossoms after an injury. Do not burn a colony during a regional die-off. Mark root-nodules but leave extraction to sealed Guild teams.

## Art direction

A ghostlike but clearly botanical silhouette; beautiful before horrifying. Emphasize felt-like petals, pale fungal textures, embedded skeletal supports, and tiny human observers for scale. Avoid a generic plant humanoid.

## Canon connections

- The Pale Orchard may be a centuries-old Bloomwraith graveyard.
- Venomvine toxins are found concentrated in mature root-nodules.
- Orchidia has been observed standing motionless among active colonies without being attacked.
