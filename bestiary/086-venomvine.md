# Bestiary No.086 — Venomvine

**Canon status:** Working canon  
**Operational class:** Class IV — Regional Crisis  
**Known range:** Widowfen and southern Mourning Reach waterways  
**Primary ecology:** Distributed predatory vine network

## Identification

Venomvine is encountered as thick blue-black cables, translucent feeding bladders, and luminous warning bulbs suspended over water. What appears to be a patch may be one connected organism extending beneath several square kilometers of wetland.

## Biology

The organism combines plantlike growth, contractile fibers, and a circulating venom slurry. Specialized bulbs sample airborne chemistry and alter toxin composition over time. Severed lengths remain reactive for days and can root after prolonged immersion.

## Behavior

Venomvine does not chase prey in the ordinary sense. It reshapes paths, narrows waterways, imitates safe ground, and waits for repeated traffic. Mature networks appear to learn migration and trade routes. During bloom events, the vines produce floating blue sacs that burst when disturbed and seed downstream colonies.

## Ecological role

It regulates large herbivore movement, creates protected breeding pools for toxin-resistant species, and captures mineral runoff. Its expansion can convert open wetland into a nearly impassable maze.

## Human relationship

Widowfen settlements harvest diluted venom for medicine, lacquer, and hunting compounds. The same trade creates pressure to preserve dangerous growth near inhabited routes. Illegal cultivators have caused multiple settlement losses.

## Field guidance

Never cut a submerged vine unless both ends are visible. Avoid routes that appear newly cleared. Blue floating sacs must be burned from a distance before they enter moving water.

## Art direction

The creature should read as an ecosystem-scale ambush predator rather than one central monster. Show huge felted cords, poisonous translucent organs, drowned architecture, and tiny boats navigating a living maze. Dominant tones: blue-black, cyan, toxic violet, wet mineral gray.

## Canon connections

- Bloomwraiths process animals killed during Venomvine bloom events.
- Orchidia may trigger or suppress blooming through unknown chemical signals.
- Widowfen's economy depends on controlled exposure to the organism.
