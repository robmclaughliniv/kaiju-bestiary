# Bestiary No.087 — Orchidia

**Canon status:** Working canon  
**Operational class:** Class Ø — Unclassifiable  
**Known range:** Violet Escarpment, Quiet Interior, disputed sightings elsewhere  
**Primary ecology:** Unknown; possible keystone signal organism

## Identification

Orchidia is a colossal orchid-like entity with a crown of layered petals, a narrow trunk-body wrapped in root veils, and long articulated limbs resembling both stems and mantis forelegs. Coloration shifts from bruised magenta to toxic green depending on humidity and bloom state.

Unlike most organisms in the Reach, Orchidia is usually seen alone.

## Biology

No confirmed tissue sample exists. Material recovered from footprints decays into ordinary pollen, mineral dust, and unidentified proteins within hours. Some records describe Orchidia as an animal wearing a flower; others describe a flower imitating an animal. Both may be inadequate.

## Behavior

Orchidia appears before major ecological transitions: mass blooming, migration reversals, sudden die-offs, and the opening of paths into the Quiet Interior. It has ignored armed expeditions, dismantled unmanned survey towers with precision, and once carried an injured child to Saint Orra's Road.

Observers exposed to its pollen frequently report vivid memories that are not their own. Guild policy classifies these accounts as contamination until independently corroborated.

## Ecological role

The leading theory is that Orchidia coordinates chemical communication between otherwise separate organisms in the Mourning Reach. A minority theory proposes the opposite: Orchidia is not coordinating the ecosystem but negotiating with it.

## Field guidance

Do not fire unless directly ordered by a Regional Curator or necessary to prevent immediate loss of life. Record wind direction, nearby bloom state, sounds, and personal memories before and after contact. Never follow Orchidia into the Quiet Interior without leaving an external chronology team.

## Art direction

Elegant, alien, regal, and poisonous. The silhouette must remain plant-dominant but not static. Use orchid anatomy, predatory limb logic, root veils, and luminous toxic accents. Orchidia should feel capable of tenderness and catastrophic violence without becoming humanoid or fairy-like.
