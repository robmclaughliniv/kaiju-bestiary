# Bestiary No.087: Okisendra

**Guild epithet:** The Blighting Rot  
**Japanese display name:** オキシデンドラ  
**Field designation:** 087-A  
**Canon status:** Established

## Classification

- **Origin:** Botanical / Corrosive
- **Guild descriptor:** Corrosive Forest Colossus
- **Disposition:** Catastrophic
- **Threat:** Existential within affected biomes
- **First verified record:** AE 2317
- **Primary habitat:** Toxic woodlands, swamps, humid lowlands

## Scale

- **Estimated height:** 122 m
- **Estimated mass:** 46,800 t
- **Locomotion:** Rooted stride using articulated vine-limbs

## Morphology

Okisendra is a walking botanical ecosystem built around a massive trunk-body crowned by a carnivorous floral structure. Its outer tissues resemble bark, fungal armor, wet roots, and layered petals. Multiple green luminescent nodules function as sensory organs, pressure glands, or spore chambers.

Its arms divide into hanging root-fingers capable of grasping trees, animals, and structures. Thorned vines spread from the shoulders and back, often dragging secondary growth behind the organism.

The body continuously leaks acidic sap and carries a dense cloud of airborne spores.

## Ecology and behavior

Okisendra does not merely destroy ecosystems. It replaces them.

As it moves, corrosive mist kills exposed plant and animal life, softens stone and metal, and prepares soil for a secondary biological bloom. Within hours, unfamiliar mosses, fungal shelves, carnivorous flowers, and root networks appear in its wake.

These successor organisms may be parts of Okisendra, dependent species, or an invasive ecology transported inside its body. Entire local food webs can collapse and be replaced before conventional containment teams arrive.

The kaiju appears to seek wet, biologically dense regions. It avoids arid terrain unless pursuing a water source.

## Recorded abilities

### Acid Spore Cloud
Releases airborne spores suspended in corrosive vapor. Exposure damages lungs, eyes, filters, optics, and untreated metal.

### Sap Jet
Projects high-pressure acidic sap over long distances.

### Vine Constriction
Entangles and crushes large prey, vehicles, trees, or defensive structures.

### Corrosion Pulse
Emits a wave of biochemical activity that accelerates rust, decay, and tissue breakdown across a broad area.

### Succession Bloom
Rapidly establishes a replacement ecosystem in chemically prepared ground.

## Guild advisory

Avoid direct entry into the mist zone. Standard respirators are insufficient. Field teams require sealed circulation, sacrificial outer plating, and independent chemical markers because visual landmarks disappear quickly.

Burning affected ground is not universally effective and may aerosolize dormant spores.

## Cultural record

Marsh communities call Okisendra the Blighting Rot. Some believe its spores are the breath of a swamp god. Others consider its passage a judgment against permanent settlement in wetlands.

A minority of Marsh Dwellers revere the successor bloom and harvest medicinal compounds from the outer edge of contaminated zones. Guild authorities strongly discourage this practice while quietly purchasing the resulting samples.
